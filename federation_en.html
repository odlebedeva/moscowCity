Federation Tower

  • Height: 373,7 m
  • Years of construction: 2004-2016
  • Developer: AEON Development
  • Total area: 443000 м2
  • Lift speed: 8 meters per second
  • Number of floors: 97
  • Number of parking space: 874
  • Infrastructure: 16159 m2
  • Sector: 13
The main feature of the Federation complex is, of course, that this is the highest structure in Europe. Even if a Gazprom Skyscraper is built in St. Petersburg above the meter (with a spire), then the Federation will still be the largest building by the number of floors (97 against 87).
The basis for the "Federation Tower" is the foundation, which is based on a massive concrete slab. 14 thousand cubic meters of concrete were spent on its filling; this achievement was recorded in the Guinness Book of Records. In the construction of the "Tower Federation", concrete is used class B90, the strength of which is twice the strength of conventional. The stability of each of them is connected with a powerful concrete core, which has a wall thickness of 1.4 meters. Also used 25 columns around the perimeter of 2 x 1.4 meters, so it is not afraid of earthquakes or collisions with aircraft. Columns are installed from the foundatiton to the last floors.
In the "Federation Tower" is panoramic glazing. The height of the opening in the platinum apartment (at the top of the tower "Vostok") reaches 5.5 meters, width - 1.83 meters. And no rails and handrails, interfering with enjoying the magnificent views of the city, sunrise and sunset. The facade of the building for the first time in Russia is realized using the technology of structural glazing with double-glazed windows filled with argon. The surface of the glass "Federation Tower" reflects ultraviolet, while maintaining the optimum temperature in the building. The density of glass is close to the parameters of heat resistance of the brick wall. On the facade of the tower "Vostok", for example, more than 9000 double-glazed windows, each of which is individual and has its own serial number. The piece approach to the production of double-glazed windows is due to two factors - first applied in Russia more by facade glazing, as well as architectural practice of the building, tapering upwards, which offers different in size facades depending on the floor.
